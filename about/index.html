aaDAC is a nonprofit network developing sustainable systems where visual artists can ideate and collaborate with other communities to incite critical change within their local spaces

Through a multi-layered curation of resources and information, which include publication of art news for our artistic community around the world and innovating programs and events; to empower cultural producers to be the initiators of critical change in their respective communities and beyond.

Our nonprofit media work to partner with cooperatives and organizations, conjuring up possibilities and systems that inform how artistic practice and the institutions that keep them can be a change catalyst for social change within the local spaces.



Team


Uche Obasi | Co-founder & Artistic director


Uche Obasi is an artist, curator and mediator. He holds a Bachelor in Art history, Fine Arts Department, University of Benin, Nigeria. Obasi had a brief role as curatorial assistant at Omenka Gallery, Nigeria; interviewing one of the leading artistic voices from the African continent, including Zimbabwean artist, Portia Zvavahera, for Omenka online and print magazine.


Chinezim Moghalu | Co-founder & Co-facilitator for projects, programs and contents


Chinezim Moghalu is an artist and critic who holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in Fine & Applied Arts, graduating in 2016 from the University of Benin, Nigeria. He majored in Art History. Moghalu has contributed critical text and articles on art to a museum of art, equally to online and print arts media.

Over the recent years, he has assisted in co-curating and facilitating a few art events at his home-country Nigeria; it includes Art X Lagos and Lagos Photo Festival.
Chinezim Moghalu nonetheless operates a studio as an art practitioner.
